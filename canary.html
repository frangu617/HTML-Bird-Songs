Canary

small songbirds that are native to the Canary Islands, an archipelago off the coast of Africa. They are about 12-14 centimeters long and weigh about 18 grams. Canaries have a yellow or green plumage, with some breeds having white or black markings. They are known for their beautiful singing, which is produced by the syrinx, a vocal organ located at the base of the trachea.

Canaries have been domesticated for centuries and are now one of the most popular cage birds in the world. They are relatively easy to care for and can live for up to 15 years. Canaries are also used in scientific research, as they have a well-understood vocal anatomy and are relatively resistant to disease.
